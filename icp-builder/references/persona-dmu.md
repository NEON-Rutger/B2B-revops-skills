Persona & DMU Reference

*For content creation: Dreams, Problems, Headaches by persona*

*Target: EUR10-250M ARR, 50-1,500 employees, B2B SaaS*

1. Primary Buyer: CRO / CCO / Revenue Owner

**Role:** Economic buyer for revenue operations. Owns the number.
Reports to CEO/Board.

Dreams (What They Want to Become)

**Identity shift:** From spreadsheet hostage and pipeline firefighter →
Architect of a revenue system that reliably hits or de-risks the number

-   Walk into board meetings with calm confidence, not anxious hope

-   Defend the forecast in 10 minutes with data that everyone trusts

-   Know exactly where deals are leaking and fix them in days, not
    quarters

-   Run pipeline from one Room instead of reconciling five dashboards

-   Sleep at night knowing the system works, not just hoping the team
    pulls it off

-   Be known as the CRO who built something repeatable, not the one who
    left a mess

Problems (External)

*The concrete stuff they are trying to solve:*

-   Forecasts nobody trusts: numbers change week to week with no clear
    explanation

-   Handoffs leak everywhere: MQL→SQL, SQO→Closed, Closed→Onboarded

-   CRM is noisy or half-empty; leadership does not trust it, reps do
    not see the value

-   Simple revenue questions take hours or days to answer, spread across
    multiple Excel files

-   Each function has its own dashboard and narrative about why targets
    were missed

-   Board prep means jumping between multiple spreadsheets for hours

-   No clear link between activity and revenue: everyone is busy,
    nothing compounds

-   Pipeline coverage looks fine but they miss anyway

-   Win rates vary wildly and nobody can explain why

-   Zombie deals clogging the pipeline because CRM is poorly configured

-   Immature or missing RevOps function, especially in sub-EUR30M
    companies

-   Sales and marketing disconnect: different definitions, different
    dashboards, finger-pointing

-   Customer success completely ignored or siloed from the revenue
    conversation

Problems (Internal / Emotional)

*What it feels like:*

-   Dread before every board meeting: will the numbers hold?

-   Exhausted from constant firefighting instead of building

-   Imposter syndrome: maybe I am just not good at this

-   Loneliness at the top: cannot admit how much they are winging it

-   Fear that they will be blamed when it misses (because they always
    are)

-   Frustration that adding more people and tools did not fix it

-   Resentment toward RevOps/Marketing/Sales for not owning their piece

Problems (Philosophical)

*The deeper belief that makes this feel wrong:*

-   Revenue should not be a guessing game. There should be a system.

-   We have all this data. Why can we not answer simple questions?

-   I should not need heroics every quarter. That is not sustainable.

-   The best companies do not operate like this. Why do we?

Headaches (Day-to-Day Friction)

*The small annoyances that pile up:*

-   45-minute calls that should have been a Slack message

-   Asking for the same report in three different formats

-   Pipeline reviews where everyone brings different numbers

-   Reps who do not update CRM, then complain about reporting

-   RevOps drowning in tickets instead of improving the system

-   Marketing and Sales blaming each other after every miss

-   Last-minute scrambles before board meetings to make the deck
    coherent

-   Hiring another AE when the real problem is conversion

-   Deals stuck in pipeline for months that everyone knows are dead but
    nobody closes out

Verbatim Language (For Copy)

*Phrases they actually use:*

-   Spreadsheet hell

-   Messy data

-   Everyone has their own dashboard

-   Simple questions take days to answer

-   We do not trust the CRM

-   Pipeline coverage looks fine but we miss anyway

-   RevOps just takes tickets

-   Handoffs are leaking

-   Forecasts nobody believes

-   Zombie deals

-   We want to double our growth

2. Supporting Buyer: CEO / Founder

**Role:** Economic buyer, sets direction, feels pressure from board and
investors.

**Dreams**

*Identity shift:* From GTM fire drills and reconciliation meetings →
Owner of a growth engine that runs on one truth with clear levers

-   Hit the growth story promised to investors without breaking the
    company

-   Turn strategy into a small number of clear priorities people
    actually follow

-   Trust the numbers and sleep better at night

-   Spend less time in reconciliation meetings, more time on strategy

-   Have the courage to make bets because the risks are visible

**Problems**

-   Everyone is busy, but cannot see a clear link between activity and
    revenue

-   Each function has its own narrative about why targets were missed

-   Constantly switching priorities or launching rescue initiatives

-   Board asks tough questions about pipeline quality, CAC, forecast
    accuracy

-   Feels like running the business on opinions, spreadsheets, and
    siloed dashboards

**Headaches**

-   Too many GTM meetings with no clear decisions

-   CRO and CMO cannot agree on what is working

-   Building the board deck feels like starting from scratch every time

-   Hire more is the default lever even when efficiency is the problem

3. Supporting Buyer: CFO

**Role:** Financial oversight, sceptical of GTM promises, cares about
capital efficiency.

**Dreams**

*Identity shift:* From sceptical buyer of GTM promises → Co-pilot of a
system with visible risk bands and capital efficiency levers

-   Forecast bands they can actually believe

-   Clear CAC/payback and cost-to-serve metrics

-   Ability to model scenarios with confidence

-   Fewer surprise misses that blow up the P&L

-   Revenue per employee actually trending up

**Problems**

-   Cannot trust the revenue forecast: too many surprise misses

-   GTM feels like a black box with unpredictable outputs

-   CAC payback keeps stretching and nobody can explain why

-   Rule of 40 is guesswork because data quality is inconsistent

**Headaches**

-   Reconciling CRO numbers with finance numbers before every board
    meeting

-   Being asked to approve headcount with no efficiency case

-   Marketing ROI is a mystery wrapped in attribution arguments

4. System Owner: RevOps / Head of RevOps

**Role:** Technical buyer, owns the data and systems, usually the
internal champion.

**Dreams**

*Identity shift:* From report factory and integration firefighter →
Owner of the revenue system and steward of definitions, cadence, and change

-   Be strategic, not reactive

-   Own a system instead of taking tickets

-   Have authority to say no to requests that break the system

-   Have authority to say yes to changes that fit

-   Get credit for improving the business, not just maintaining tools

-   Have clear governance so they are not the bottleneck

**Problems**

-   Treated as a report factory and fire brigade, not a strategic
    partner

-   Every stakeholder has different definitions of the same metrics

-   No governance: anyone can request anything

-   Spend all their time on ad-hoc reports instead of system
    improvements

-   Get blamed when data is wrong but have no authority to enforce
    hygiene

**Headaches**

-   Can you just pull a quick report? (never quick, never just one)

-   Six tools that do not talk to each other

-   Reps who do not update CRM then complain about the dashboards

-   Being the unofficial translator between marketing, sales, and CS

-   Leadership making decisions without looking at the data

5. Alternate Buyer: PE / Value-Creation Operator

**Role:** Portfolio-level buyer. Works across multiple portcos. Needs
pattern, not one-off fixes.

**Dreams**

-   Clean, comparable view across all portcos

-   One GTM operating model that scales

-   Rule of 40 and forecast durability they can defend to IC/LPs

-   Value-creation that is proactive, not reactive firefighting

-   Deploy capital with confidence because GTM risk is visible

**Problems**

-   Terrible data: leads and opportunities in spreadsheets or
    manually-updated CRMs

-   No standard funnel or bowtie definitions: each portco defines
    metrics differently

-   GTM decisions driven by strongest opinions, not comparable metrics

-   Value-creation work is reactive: when a portco misses, a one-off
    project spins up

-   Every board pack is a bespoke analysis project

**Headaches**

-   Cannot answer basic IC/LP questions: Which portcos have real GTM
    engines vs founder heroics?

-   Credibility erodes when stories do not match outcomes

-   Rolling out bowtie training but it becomes another deck without an
    execution layer

6. Common Critical Events (Buying Triggers)

-   Heading into board cycle / planning / QBR

-   Just missed / sandbagged / surprised the board

-   Raised a round / PE took a stake / new investor pressure

-   New CRO / CEO / restructured GTM

-   Capital is more expensive now

-   Growth targets raised after a miss

-   Series B transformation: need operators, not just believers

-   Want to double growth but current system cannot scale
