# Positioning → Messaging Framework Reference

## The Core Problem: The Copy Skip

Most companies follow this broken path:
- ICP → (skip) → Copy
- Result: rewriting forever. Copy is decoration, not strategy.

The missing piece: **Positioning + Messaging Framework**.

## The Chain: ICP → Messaging → Copy

| Stage | Input | Output | Purpose |
|-------|-------|--------|---------|
| **ICP + SPICED** | Market research, interviews | WHO they are, WHAT they feel | Audience definition |
| **Positioning** | ICP + competitive context | WHERE you stand in their mind | Market perception |
| **Messaging Framework** | Positioning + Use Case Canvas | WHAT to say (structured, reusable) | Source of truth |
| **Copy** | Messaging Framework | Every channel (LinkedIn, web, email, sales) | Execution |

**Key insight**: Skip the messaging framework, and copy becomes decoration. Build it right, and copy becomes inevitable.

---

## 1. Positioning — 12 Elements to Consider

Positioning is not a tagline. It's the structure that makes your solution visible in a crowded market.

### Primary Positioning Elements

These answer the immediate question: "Why should I choose you?"

| Element | Definition | Example (B2B GTM) |
|---------|-----------|-------------------|
| **Category** | What market do you play in? | "Marketing strategy & execution platform" |
| **Alternative** | What do they compare you to? | "Hiring freelancers or big agencies" |
| **Differentiation** | What makes you meaningfully different? | "No-code, repeatable workflows -- vs. ad-hoc work" |
| **Capabilities** | What can they DO with you that they can't elsewhere? | "Launch 10 campaigns a week without hiring" |
| **Benefits** | What results do they get? | "Faster go-to-market, lower cost per acquisition" |
| **Features** | What powers those capabilities? | "Pre-built templates, workflow automation, analytics" |

### Secondary Positioning Elements

These add specificity and defensibility.

| Element | Definition | Relevance |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| **Personas** | Who specifically benefits most? | Narrows focus (e.g., "CMOs at B2B SaaS") |
| **Use Case** | What job are they hiring you for? | "Building repeatable GTM processes" |
| **Overarching Problem** | What big-picture struggle ties it all together? | "Marketing can't scale without hiring" |
| **Sub-Problems (1, 2, 3)** | What specific blockers do they face? | Inconsistent messaging across campaigns; Long launch cycles; Campaign quality relies on individuals |

## 2. Functional Description Formula — 6 Types of Positioning

Use one of these as your single, repeatable positioning line. Buyers recognize it fast. It works across proposals, LinkedIn, and sales calls.

### 1. **Problem/Outcome-led**
*Structure*: "We help [who] reduce [pain] so they achieve [result]."

*Example*: "We help B2B SaaS CMOs reduce campaign launch time by 70% so they ship GTM campaigns faster than competitors."

*When to use*: Your main value is solving a pain that's costly or time-consuming.

---

### 2. **Job-to-be-Done**
*Structure*: "When you need to [job], use [us] instead of [old way]."

*Example*: "When you need to build repeatable GTM playbooks, use our platform instead of hiring new marketers."

*When to use*: The job is clear and well-defined; you're the obvious alternative.

---

### 3. **Category + Competitor**
*Structure*: "Unlike [alternative], we're [difference]."

*Example*: "Unlike hiring freelancers, we give you repeatable, team-owned processes."

*When to use*: You're competing for budget/mindshare against a specific rival.

---

### 4. **Segment/Niche-led**
*Structure*: "[Category/product] built for [niche], not generic [everyone]."

*Example*: "GTM strategy platform built for B2B SaaS teams, not generic marketing departments."

*When to use*: You own a specific niche and don't try to appeal to everyone.

---

### 5. **Primary Value Vector**
*Structure*: "The most [value angle] way for [who] to [result]."

*Example*: "The fastest way for B2B SaaS marketers to go from strategy to execution."

*When to use*: Your primary differentiator is speed, cost, simplicity, or scale.

---

### 6. **Strategic Archetype**
*Structure*: "The [archetype] in [category] for [who] who need [job]."

*Example*: "The operating system for B2B SaaS GTM teams who need to ship campaigns at scale."

*When to use*: You're positioning as a foundational platform or system, not a tool.

---

**Pick ONE. Make it repeatable. Back it with proof. Keep it consistent across all channels.**

## 3. The Use Case Messaging Canvas

The canvas has two sides: **The OLD Way** (what they do now) and **The NEW Way** (what becomes possible with you), connected by a central Use Case and Persona.

### Canvas Structure

```
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                          CURRENT WAY (LEFT)                                  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------+
|                                                                              |
|  OVERARCHING PROBLEM                    USE CASE                             |
|  (Big Picture Struggle)                 (What they are trying to do)         |
|  In their words, from interviews.       The job/outcome, clearly defined.    |
|                                                                              |
|  PROBLEM (3 Specific Pains)             PERSONA                              |
|  Word-for-word from interviews.         Who specifically benefits most.       |
|  (This is the "P" in SPICED)            Names, titles, behaviors.            |
|                                                                              |
|  LIMITATION OF CURRENT WAY              NEW WAY (RIGHT SIDE BEGINS HERE) --+ |
|  (Anti-Feature)                         (Product Capability)              |  |
|  What's broken about how they           What becomes possible.            |  |
|  do it now.                             (Opposite of left).               |  |
|                                                                           |  |
|  CURRENT WAY                            PRODUCT FEATURE                   |  |
|  (Anti-Capability)                      (What powers the ability)         |  |
|  What they actually do today.           Features serve capabilities,      |  |
|  (This is the "C" in SPICED)            don't lead with them.             |  |
|                                                                           |  |
|                                         BENEFIT                           |  |
|                                         (Result of using the ability)     |  |
|                                         Directly answers a Pain.          |  |
|                                                                           |  |
|                                         DESIRED OUTCOME                   |  |
|                                         (Main goal)                       |  |
|                                         The aspirational end state.       |  |
|                                                                           |  |
+-----------------------------------------------------------------------------+
```

### Left Side — The Current Way (Their Reality)

**Overarching Problem** (Big Picture Struggle)
- The market-level pain, in their words
- From interviews and SPICED analysis
- Example: "Marketing teams can't scale GTM without hiring more people."

**Problem** (3 Specific Blockers)
- Pain 1: from SPICED interviews (exact language)
- Pain 2: from SPICED interviews (exact language)
- Pain 3: from SPICED interviews (exact language)
- These are the "P" in SPICED; use their exact words

**Limitation of Current Way** (Anti-Feature)
- What's broken about how they do it now
- Example: "Campaign launches take weeks because each one is custom-built."

**Current Way** (Anti-Capability)
- What they actually do today (often manual, time-consuming, fragile)
- This is the "C" in SPICED
- Example: "Build campaigns from scratch each time, relying on individual expertise."

### Center — The Job & Persona

**Use Case** (What they are trying to do)
- The specific job or outcome
- Example: "Build repeatable GTM playbooks that the team can execute without hiring."

**Persona** (Who specifically)
- Name, title, behaviors, context
- Example: "Sarah, VP Marketing at a B2B SaaS company with $5-50M ARR"

### Right Side — The New Way (Your Solution)

**New Way** (Product Capability)
- Each capability is the OPPOSITE of a Current Way step
- What becomes possible
- Example: "Use pre-built, customizable campaign templates that launch in days."

**Product Feature** (What powers the ability)
- The specific technical or product lever
- Features serve capabilities; don't lead with features
- Example: "Drag-and-drop workflow builder with 50+ pre-built campaign templates"

**Benefit** (Result of using the ability)
- Directly solves a Pain from the left side
- Example: "Save 3 weeks per campaign launch"

**Desired Outcome** (Main goal)
- The aspirational end state
- Example: "Ship 10 GTM campaigns a quarter without hiring"

## 4. The Opposites Method

**The secret**: Every New Way element is the OPPOSITE of an Old Way element.

This is how the canvas *writes your messaging for you*.

| Current Way (Left) | → Opposite → | New Way (Right) |
|-------------------|-------------|-----------------|
| "Campaign launches take weeks" | → | "Launch campaigns in days" |
| "Relies on one expert's knowledge" | → | "Repeatable for anyone on the team" |
| "Each campaign is custom-built" | → | "Use proven templates and workflows" |
| "Time is the blocker" | → | "Speed is the advantage" |
| "Cost goes up with volume" | → | "Cost stays flat as you scale" |

### How to Fill the Canvas

1. **Start with SPICED Analysis**
   - Interview customers, map their world
   - Capture exact language: Problems (P), Critical Events (C), Ideal state (I), etc.

2. **Fill the Left Side First**
   - This is THEIR reality, not your narrative
   - Use their words from interviews
   - If it doesn't come from SPICED, it's not positioning -- it's fiction

3. **Flip Each Row to the Right**
   - Problem → Benefit (the benefit directly solves the pain)
   - Anti-feature → Product Feature (what was broken → what's now possible)
   - Anti-capability → Capability (what they did manually → what the system does)

4. **The Opposites Write Your Messaging**
   - You don't invent messaging
   - You extract it from the contrast between Old and New

## 5. From Framework to Copy

The messaging framework is **NOT a document you write once and file away**. It's the **operating system for everything your team says**.

Your messaging appears in:
- LinkedIn posts and articles
- Website copy (homepage, product pages, case studies)
- Sales decks and pitches
- Email campaigns
- Customer calls
- Proposal templates

**Three tools working together:**

| Tool | Purpose | Feeds Into |
|------|---------|-----------|
| **SPICED** | WHAT to say (the substance) | Use Case Canvas |
| **Use Case Canvas** | WHERE to put it (structure) | Copy outlines |
| **Opposites** | HOW to say it (the contrast) | Every headline, email, post |

**Result**: One canvas. One source of truth. Copy writes itself.

## 6. Story Building — The Weekly GTM Sequence

A practical 3-day workflow to build positioning and messaging from real customer evidence.

### Tuesday: ICP + Personas
**Goal**: Map your best-fit customer from real deal evidence

**Activities**:
- Pull last 5 closed deals (biggest deals, happiest customers)
- For each deal: Extract company size, role, industry, approval chain
- SPICED ICP creation: Interview or review notes
  - **S**ituation: Where do they work? What do they do?
  - **P**ain: What's keeping them up at night?
  - **I**deal state: What would "solved" look like?
  - **C**ritical event: What triggered the search for a solution?
  - **E**conomic buyer: Who owns the budget?
  - **D**ecision criteria: How do they evaluate options?

**Output**: Ideal Customer Profile + 2-3 key personas with names, titles, and SPICED attributes

---

### Wednesday: Position Yourself
**Goal**: Map positioning from best-customer language and competitive context

**Activities**:
- From Tuesday's ICP: Extract category, alternative, differentiation
- Interview customers: "What were you comparing us to?"
- Fill in Primary Positioning Elements (category, alternative, diff, capabilities, benefits, features)
- Write ONE positioning line (use the 6 formulas above)
- Test it: Does it resonate with your best customers? Can sales say it naturally?

**Output**: Single positioning line + defensible rationale, backed by customer language

---

### Thursday: Messaging
**Goal**: Map messaging from SPICED ICP + positioning. Validate through content, calls, outreach.

**Activities**:
- From Tuesday's SPICED: Fill the LEFT SIDE of the Use Case Canvas (their reality)
- From Wednesday's positioning: Fill the RIGHT SIDE (your solution)
- Use the Opposites method to ensure every new way element counters an old way pain
- Test messaging: Use it in 3 outreach emails or calls -- listen for resonance
- The two sides of a messaging framework become content: "Old Way vs. New Way" posts are high-engagement

**Output**: Complete Use Case Canvas + first 5 pieces of messaging-backed content

## 7. Usage Guide

### When to Use This Reference

- **AFTER** building the ICP (see icp-builder skill)
- **BEFORE** writing any sales collateral, website copy, or content calendar
- **DURING** launch planning and competitive positioning discussions

### How to Use Each Section

| Section | Use Case | Who |
|---------|----------|-----|
| **6 Positioning Formulas** | Write your LinkedIn headline, one-liner for proposals, elevator pitch | Founders, marketing lead |
| **Use Case Canvas** | Structure customer deliverables (enablement, case studies); source of truth for messaging | GTM team, copywriter |
| **Opposites Method** | Generate LinkedIn content (high engagement); ensure consistency across channels | Content creator, social team |
| **Primary Positioning Elements** | Brief sales team and ensure shared language | Sales manager, marketing |
| **Weekly GTM Sequence** | Run recurring positioning workshops; build messaging artifacts | Growth lead, full team |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Writing positioning before SPICED** → Guessing instead of listening
2. **Filling the right side before the left** → Your narrative overwrites their reality
3. **Using "nice" words instead of customer words** → Messaging doesn't resonate
4. **Forgetting the Opposites** → New way doesn't clearly contrast with old way
5. **Creating a document instead of a system** → Canvas collects dust; copy doesn't improve
