# RevOps Crisis Playbook Templates
**For: B2B scale-ups €15M–150M ARR**

Crisis response playbooks with structured timelines for rapid mitigation and recovery.

---

## 1. PIPELINE CRISIS PLAYBOOK
**Trigger:** Pipeline coverage falls below 2x (e.g., Q1 at risk mid-quarter)

### First 24 Hours: Emergency Response
- **War room activation:** CRO, VP Sales, RevOps, key team leads
- **Forensic analysis:** Identify drop cause (deals slipped, lower deal value, lower win rate, lower inbound, rep changes)
- **Sales calls:** Deal-by-deal triage of stalled deals; identify immediate movers
- **Forecast adjustment:** Update forecast, flag board-level risk
- **Contingency pipeline activation:** Activate existing pipeline from next quarter

### First Week: Pipeline Blitz
- **Deal acceleration:** Negotiate earlier close dates on Best Case pipeline
- **Win-back campaigns:** Prioritize lost deals from past 6 months with discounting authority
- **Outbound blitz:** Sales team 40% time on new prospecting; SDR coverage increase
- **Partner activation:** Activate channel partners for quick-win opportunities
- **Pricing flexibility:** Authorize quarterly/annual discount for Q-close acceleration
- **Weekly war room:** Every 2 days initially to track coverage recovery

### First Month: Demand Gen Rebuild
- **Paid campaign acceleration:** Increase ad spend on high-intent keywords
- **Marketing event sprint:** Webinars, roundtables, partner events to generate meetings
- **Sales development focus:** Resume normal SDR activity after emergency blitz
- **Qualification tightening:** Ensure new leads meet ICP to prevent false recovery
- **Pipeline health:** Track new pipeline quality, win rates, deal sizes

### Ongoing: Prevention
- **Pipeline visibility:** Weekly coverage reports to leadership; flagging at 2.5x threshold
- **Forecast discipline:** Bi-weekly forecast calibration; deal assessment rigor
- **Conversion funnel monitoring:** Track source → qualified → pipeline conversion by channel
- **Rep capacity planning:** Ensure territory management prevents coverage gaps
- **Demand gen baseline:** Establish minimum new inbound by source to maintain pipeline

## 2. FORECAST MISS PLAYBOOK
**Trigger:** Q at risk of missing guidance mid-quarter (>10% gap)

### First 24 Hours: Triage and Stabilization
- **Board notification:** Inform CFO/CEO of potential miss; define disclosure timeline
- **Deal audit:** Classify all Commit pipeline:
  - Tier 1 (highly likely to close): quantify
  - Tier 2 (possible): identify conditions needed
  - Tier 3 (unlikely): flag for removal
- **Best Case assessment:** Pull forward realistic Best Case deals into Commit
- **Contingency activation:** What's the minimum revenue needed and from where?
- **Leadership alignment:** Establish daily standup (CRO, CFO, RevOps)

### First Week: Acceleration and Contingency
- **Commit deal deep-dives:** CRO personally involved in top 10 deals; customer calls
- **Best Case conversion:** Identify 5–10 deals with acceleration potential; negotiate close dates
- **Contingency pipeline:** Activate pipeline from Q+1; calculate realistic probability
- **Win-back urgency:** Fast-track lost deals from last 60 days
- **Customer upsell/expansion:** CS team identifies expansion revenue; accelerate closes
- **Board communication:** Weekly updates on progress; set clear miss vs. recovery scenario

### First Month: Sustained Effort
- **Weekly deal review:** Monday-Wednesday Commit-only reviews; focus on deal progression
- **Rep accountability:** Identify reps below target; provide support or escalation
- **Forecast accuracy:** Strict deal-stage definitions; no deals in Commit without executive sign-off
- **Expansion focus:** Lean into existing customer expansion to offset new logo shortfall
- **Replan planning:** Begin Q+1 pipeline planning to prevent recurring misses

### Post-Miss: Root Cause Resolution
- **Post-mortem:** Sales, Marketing, RevOps review actual vs. forecast
- **Process remediation:** Tighten forecast discipline (deal stage definitions, review cadence, review depth)
- **Compensation review:** If miss was predictable, consider pipeline/forecast metrics for comp
- **Demand gen investment:** Assess whether new business pipeline investment needed
- **Retention focus:** Ensure customer retention/expansion is planned as forecast buffer

## 3. CHURN SPIKE PLAYBOOK
**Trigger:** Monthly churn exceeds historical threshold (e.g., 5% for B2B SaaS)

### First 24 Hours: Immediate Response
- **Churn forensics:** Identify churned accounts by segment, reason, contract value, tenure
- **Risk segmentation:** Tier at-risk accounts by revenue impact (high ARR at-risk = Tier 1)
- **War room activation:** CS lead, Account Exec, Product, RevOps
- **Escalation list:** Create list of top 20 at-risk accounts by retention value
- **Customer calls:** Executive outreach (VP/Director level) to Tier 1 at-risk accounts
- **Early warning system:** Flag accounts showing warning signs (low engagement, support tickets, lack of login)

### First Week: Save Plays
- **Executive check-ins:** CRO calls to top customers at risk; listen for product/support gaps
- **Product review:** Product team reviews churn feedback; identify quick-fix vs. roadmap items
- **Success plan adjustment:** CSM updates success plans for at-risk accounts; focus on value realization
- **Win-back offers:** For recent churns, create targeted win-back incentive (25–50% for 3-month trial)
- **Engagement campaign:** Launch email/webinar campaign to re-engage low-usage segments
- **Root cause categorization:** Group reasons (product gaps, support issues, budget cuts, competitive loss, internal change)

### First Month: Structural Prevention
- **Customer feedback loop:** Weekly review of churn feedback with Product
- **Support improvements:** If support is driver, add capacity or improve response time
- **Customer health tracking:** Implement health score model; monthly review with CS team
- **Pricing/packaging review:** If price-driven, assess competitive positioning and packaging options
- **Cohort analysis:** Track churn by segment, vintage, product tier; identify highest-risk groups

### Ongoing: Churn Reduction Program
- **Prevention metrics:** Track health score, NPS, engagement metrics by account
- **CS resource allocation:** Ensure CS capacity matches at-risk account load
- **Playbook refinement:** Develop segment-specific churn playbooks (SMB vs. Enterprise, by use case)
- **Win-back program:** Quarterly win-back campaign for recent churns
- **Product roadmap alignment:** Ensure product is tracking customer needs of at-risk segments

## 4. REP ATTRITION CRISIS PLAYBOOK
**Trigger:** Multiple reps depart (>2 in 30 days); vacancy rate exceeds 15%

### First 24 Hours: Damage Control
- **Territory assessment:** Map out which territories lose coverage; impact on pipeline
- **Pipeline protection:** Identify critical deals in departing reps' territories
- **Reassignment protocol:** Assign critical pipeline to strong closers to protect Q revenue
- **Customer communication:** Account leadership reaches out to customers; ensure continuity
- **Exit interviews:** Conduct with departing reps; understand why they left
- **Retention conversations:** If pattern is identified, retain flight-risk reps now

### First Week: Coverage and Hiring
- **Emergency coverage:** Rotate reps to backfill high-value territories
- **Sourcing blitz:** Activate recruiter for expedited hiring; consider contract reps as interim
- **Territory adjustment:** Expand quota for covering reps only if temporary (max 3 months)
- **Bonus pool:** Consider temporary retention bonus for reps covering multiple territories
- **Inbound workload:** Ensure SDR team targets backfilled territories to ease rep burden
- **Manager support:** Ensure frontline managers have resources to support coverage

### First Month: Hiring and Knowledge Transfer
- **Accelerated recruiting:** Target competitors for fast placement; offer premium comp
- **Interim coverage:** Hire contract sales reps to backfill while permanent hiring in process
- **Knowledge documentation:** Capture departing reps' strategies, relationships, deal tactics
- **Shadowing program:** Pair new hires with experienced reps for rapid onboarding
- **Manager load:** Ensure reps covering multiple territories have reduced other responsibilities
- **Quota adjustment:** Only backfilled reps carry reduced quota during coverage period

### Ongoing: Retention and Stability
- **Root cause analysis:** Understand why reps left (comp, culture, growth, management)
- **Comp review:** If market-based, adjust base/variable to stay competitive
- **Manager effectiveness:** Assess quality of frontline leadership; invest in coaching if needed
- **Career pathing:** Create clear paths to AE → team lead → manager for high performers
- **Culture audit:** If multiple departures, assess team health and management quality
- **Turnover target:** Establish goal (<15% annual AE turnover); track against goals

## 5. DATA CRISIS PLAYBOOK
**Trigger:** CRM data integrity compromised (bad import, system sync failure, process breakdown)

### First 24 Hours: Containment
- **Impact assessment:** Scope the data problem (which objects, date range, affected records)
- **Automation freeze:** Pause all CRM automations to prevent data propagation errors
- **Affected systems:** Identify downstream systems impacted (forecasting tools, reporting, syncs)
- **War room activation:** RevOps, CRM admin, IT, key users
- **Communication:** Alert users not to rely on questionable data; pause dependent processes
- **Root cause:** Identify how corruption occurred (user error, system sync, bad integration, migration issue)

### First Week: Audit and Remediation
- **Data audit:** Sample affected records; quantify scope of corruption
- **Remediation plan:** Determine if data can be corrected or if rollback needed
- **Rollback assessment:** If major, consider restoring from backup (timing and impact)
- **Manual correction:** If piecemeal, prioritize corrections by revenue impact
- **Automation testing:** Test all automations before re-enabling; validate logic
- **Process review:** Identify process breakdown that allowed corruption
- **User retraining:** If user error caused it, retrain on correct process

### First Month: Validation and Recovery
- **Data quality checks:** Run validation rules on previously affected data
- **Downstream reconciliation:** Validate impacted reports, forecasts, analyses
- **System sync validation:** Test integrations that connect to CRM; ensure no ongoing corruption
- **Process hardening:** Add controls, field validations, user training to prevent recurrence
- **Stakeholder communication:** Share what happened, what's fixed, how it's prevented going forward
- **Metrics restoration:** Regenerate reports once data integrity confirmed

### Ongoing: Prevention
- **CRM governance:** Define data stewardship, approval workflows for bulk changes
- **Access controls:** Limit who can edit critical fields; require approval for bulk updates
- **Validation rules:** Implement field-level validation to prevent bad data entry
- **Audit trails:** Maintain audit logs of major data changes
- **Regular audits:** Monthly data quality checks on critical fields (amounts, forecast, dates)
- **Backup discipline:** Regular backups with documented recovery procedures
- **Integration monitoring:** Monitor syncs between CRM and other systems for data conflicts

## Quick Reference: When to Activate War Room
| Crisis Type | Revenue Impact | Timeline | War Room? |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pipeline crisis (coverage <2x) | Q at risk | Mid-quarter | **YES** |
| Forecast miss (>10% gap) | Q at risk | Mid-quarter | **YES** |
| Churn spike (>threshold) | ARR bleed | Ongoing | Maybe (if >€100k/mo) |
| Rep attrition (>2 reps) | Territory coverage risk | Ongoing | Yes (if >3 reps) |
| Data crisis (system failure) | Visibility lost | Urgent | **YES** |
