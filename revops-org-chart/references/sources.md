# RevOps Org Chart — Source Reference

## 1. RevOps Team Roles and Hiring Guide
**Type:** Internal reference
**Created:** November 2025

Four org chart tiers + role breakdowns:

**1–3 people (Foundation):** Director of RevOps (first hire, 5+ years, strong generalist), RevOps Analyst (junior, data-comfortable), Systems/CRM Admin (owns GTM tech stack and integrations).

**4–6 people (Growth):** Triggered at 50–60 CRM end users. Adds: second CRM Admin, RevOps Manager (PM + people manager, runs weekly sprints), additional Analyst, BI/Insights Manager (Looker/Snowflake/Tableau), Revenue Enablement Manager.

**7–10 people (Scale):** Manager shifts to Program Manager (generalist, manages up to executives). Three analysts (one per funnel segment: Sales, Marketing, CS). Marketing Operations Lead (3+ years RevOps, 2+ years Marketing Ops, Marketo/HubSpot admin). CS Operations Lead (3+ years RevOps, CS experience preferred). These are the proto-Business Partner roles.

**10+ people (Mature):** All prior roles with deeper specialization. Clear swim lanes across funnel. Requires explicit governance.

## 2. RevOps Co-op: Evolutionary Org Chart
**URL:** https://www.revopscoop.com/post/how-should-your-revenue-operations-organization-be-structured
**Also on:** https://revopscoop.substack.com/p/revops-co-op-weekly-18-how-should
**Format:** Four-stage evolution diagram

Stage 1 — Siloed: Ops report to department VPs. Gets deprioritized. Systems Architects exist but have no mandate.
Stage 2 — Departmental: All ops under one manager, but organized by department they support. Cross-team conflicts emerge.
Stage 3 — Functional Specialization: Reorganized by capability. Systems team (Architects + Admins) and Insights team (Analysts) each get dedicated managers. Manager of Systems team = former Systems Architect.
Stage 4 — Evolved: Enablement joins. Cross-functional PMs and Analysts. Agile methodologies. Ride-alongs (ops shadows end users quarterly).

**Key quote paraphrased:** Hiring a manager who has been a systems architect to manage the systems team just makes sense.

## 4. Hyperscayle: Maturity Model (5 Levels)
**URL:** https://hyperscayle.com/insights/revops-framework-team-structure
**Also:** https://hyperscayle.com/insights/revops-maturity-model

Level 1 — Ad Hoc: No dedicated ops.
Level 2 — Functional: 1–2 analysts keeping things running.
Level 3 — Repeatable: RevOps Director + 3–4 analysts. Minimum cost ~$600K/year.
Level 4 — Scalable: VP RevOps + multiple Directors (Marketing Ops, Sales Ops, CS Ops, PM).
Level 5 — Optimized: Full mature organization.

Most common under-investment: RevOps team itself.
Most common over-investment: Systems and tools.
Key principle: Right-size the team with the complexity of the GTM motion, not ARR.

## 5. Go Nimbly: Three Structural Models
**URL:** https://gonimbly.com/blog/revops/revops-org-structure-a-comprehensive-guide/

**Departmental Model:** Sales Ops, Marketing Ops, CS Ops as separate teams under Head of RevOps. Best for early-stage. Risk: re-siloing.

**Functional Model:** Strategy, Tools/Data, Enablement, Insights teams — each serve all departments. Best for mature orgs. This is the Center of Excellence approach.

**Flat Structure:** Minimal hierarchy, empowered ICs. Startups only. Does not scale.
